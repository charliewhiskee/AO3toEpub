“What is going on?”  Jones asks once the door closes.  “I thought I was done being in here,'' he mumbles, leaning back in his seat and looking at Tony.

 

“Just double-checking,” Tony answers, mirroring Jones and leaning back.  “Where were you last Tuesday night?” he asks, watching him.

 

Jones sighs.  “I walked home after I had been to the gym.  That’s it.”

 

“That path would bring you past Rock Creek Park, wouldn’t it?”

 

“Yeah… Yes, I usually take a shortcut through the park on my way home,” Jones agrees, shifting to sit more upright.

 

“At what time did you leave the gym?”

 

“As it closed, at 8.”  Jones shrugs one shoulder.  “I usually stay till closing.”

 

The door creaks open, and Burley re-enters the room.  “The results are in, so why don’t you just tell us the truth?” He asks Jones as he takes a seat next to Tony.

 

“I am telling you the truth.”  Jones leans forward, his lower arms on the table.

 

“When and how did you get hurt?” Tony asks, nodding to the bandage right below Jones’ left elbow.

 

The question surprises Jones, and he hurries to cover up the bandage with the sleeve of his shirt.  “Oh- oh, that was just a work accident, last Wednesday. Nothing major.”

 

“We have footage of you leaving the gym alongside Petty Officer Boswell the night he died.  Want to explain that?” Burley asks him.

 

“So we both left as the gym closed.  You would have seen that other people left at the same time.”

 

“You and Petty Officer Boswell were the only ones who didn’t walk around to the parking lot nearby.  Instead, you both headed towards Rock Creek Park,” Burley continues calmly.

 

“It’s on my way home, of course I would go that way.  We just discussed this, didn’t we? I didn’t stab Thomas.” Jones begins to sound a bit agitated, and he lightly tugs on his shirt sleeve.

 

Tony and Burley glance at each other, before Burley produces a crime scene photo of Boswell’s body, sliding it across the table.  “Petty Officer Boswell was fatally stabbed on the night of the 24th of this month in Rock Creek Park,” Tony begins. “We never disclosed the way he died.”

 

One could see the way Jones pales, and he looks down at the photo as he swallows shakily.  “I- I guess there’s no way around it now, is it? I’m not sorry I did it, nobody found out who hurt Amanda, and nobody was doing anything to find it out either.”

 

Burley moves over to Jones.  “Keith Taylor Jones, you are arrested for the murder of Naval Petty Officer Thomas Evan Boswell.  You have the right to stay silent, but anything you do say may be used against you in court. You have the right to an attorney...”  As he speaks, Tony stands up and opens the door, gesturing for the agent outside to take his place. Tony leaves the room without looking back.

 

*~*~*~*~*

 

As Jones was being taken away, Tony went out to get himself another cup of coffee, and ordered one for Gibbs as well.  He brings these two cups up to the bullpen, being careful to keep them apart. He hands the one he hasn’t been sipping to Gibbs, not surprised when his husband grabs it a bit too eagerly, and Tony watches as the first sip is taken.

 

“What is this?!” Gibbs asks, scowling, first at the cup and then at Tony.  “This isn’t coffee.” Tony’s eyes widen, and he takes a half-step back in defense.

 

“I swear, I got you your usual, Gibbs.”

 

“Well, this for sure ain’t it, so I don’t know what you did to it.”  Gibbs’ eyes are hard on Tony, making the agent visibly uncomfortable.

 

“I didn’t touch it, I don’t know.  I double-checked with Monica, too, to make sure you got the right order.”  His voice is strained and pleading to be believed. Suddenly, something clicks in the back of Tony’s head, but he doesn’t mention anything about it.  “We have to have a talk with Vance. Just toss the coffee if you don’t want it.”  

 

Gibbs scowls at his coffee again and drops it into the closest trash can before following Tony towards the director’s office.

 

*~*~*~*~*

 

“Come in.”  Leon Vance watches the door open to Tony and Gibbs, and Tony closes the door before they approach the director, walking as one.

 

“Fornell called”, Gibbs tells him before Vance can ask.  “The signature in the coding has been recognized as one Elf Lord.”

 

“Tim McGee used this name as his online presence”, Tony fills in.  “Which means it was as we suspected, McGee was the one who tried to gain access to our files.”

 

Vance sighs and runs a hand across his mouth.  “Okay. So he did that.”

 

“It wouldn’t surprise us if it was discovered that Abby Sciuto was a part of this as well,” Tony continues.  “We all know how close they are and how much Abby has been his driving force.”

 

Vance nods.  “I’ll call Garcia personally and ask her to locate the laptop.  The information will be relayed directly to you, Gibbs, once they try something else.”

 

“Thank you, Director,” Gibbs says.

 

“But keep me informed, I want to know what they’re up to.”  Vance looks at Gibbs pointedly. “If that’s all, you’re free to go.”

 

“Thank you for your time, Director”, Tony smiles and turns to head for the door, and after two creaks of the door, Vance picks up his phone and types in a number, tapping his fingers on his desk absently.

 

*~*~*~*~*

 

Now alone in the conference room once again, Roberts prepares to continue going through McGee’s book and the case files, only to discover that the touch sensitivity of the tablet isn’t as it used to be.  He needs to press down a lot harder to achieve the same things that had worked perfectly before lunch. He tries to restart the device, and while he does that, he moves onto his laptop.

 

He could have sworn he hadn’t left the devices alone, but the rolling speed of the computer mouse was a lot faster than it had previously been.  Precision is difficult as he locates the settings to reset the mouse controls, but just as he hits save changes , the tablet springs back to life.  Testingly, Roberts touches the screen to unlock it, only to find that nothing had changed there.  A sigh is hidden by the hand he drags across his mouth before he seeks out the settings, perhaps a bit more violently than need be, but he knows he really doesn’t have time for this.  Roberts turns the touch sensitivity to a level he can work with before settling back into his previous routine, considering which of the team members he could blame for the scheme.

 

*~*~*~*~*

 

Tony and Gibbs part ways at the bottom of the stairs, with Gibbs heading towards the bullpen while Tony heads to the conference room where Roberts is working.  He knocks on the door before opening it.

 

“Making any progress?”  Tony asks as he steps in, making sure to shut the door behind him.

 

“Some, but I got distracted by the fact that during lunch, the laptop and tablet I was working with got tampered with.”

 

“Tampered with?” Tony asks, furrowing his brows.  “They were never left alone, were they?”

 

“Oh, not like that.  No progress was lost or anything.  The mouse scrolling speed and tablet touch sensitivity were altered, but I couldn’t find anything to suggest anyone had tampered with the information on either device.”

 

Tony nods, relieved.  “Okay, good. You manage to fix it?”

 

“Yeah, it didn’t take long.”  Roberts watches Tony, and judges that he wasn’t the one who’d done it.

 

“Alright, good.  How much further have you gotten?”

 

“Not much, there’s so much wrong to pick apart about this book.  And then there’s the sequel.”

 

Tony sighs.  “Yeah, the sequel.”  He shakes his head and turns to leave.  “Have a nice evening, Roberts.”

 

“You too, Tony.”

 

*~*~*~*~*

When Gibbs returns to the bullpen, Quinn looks up at him.  “It was quick, Jones didn’t resist and confessed. He’s being processed right now to be taken away,” she tells him.  Gibbs nods and heads for his desk, moving to sit down just as Ducky appears at the end of the bullpen.

 

Ducky smiles at Gibbs before looking over at Tony’s empty desk.  In his hand is a tiny item that occasionally gives out a faint beep alongside a soft, red light.  He stops beside Tony’s desk and waits for him.  Once Tony joins them, he smiles at Ducky. “Hey, Ducky, what can I do for you?”  He asks as he sits down. 

 

“Now, Anthony, I know of your love for Halloween and its pranks, however, this thing has been bothering me all day, and I have been unable to be of much use today because of its presence on the inside of my lampshade.”  Ducky’s voice is stern, but not angry, as he holds the small widget out for Tony to take.

 

“Wh- I’m sorry, what?” Tony asks, looking deeply confused as he examines the item he was handed.  “I don’t know anything about - Gibbs said you’d been pranked during lunch, but I didn’t… oh ,” he breathes out a soft sound as he realizes what he’s looking at.  “This is one of those new Annoy-a-trons, right? I had heard they were lightweight, but that they were able to stick to a lampshade -”  The slight grin Tony is wearing freezes and falls as he notices Ducky’s unimpressed expression. “Sorry, Ducky, but I really didn’t put it in your office.  I haven’t even been to your office today.”

 

“Of course I wish to believe you, Anthony, but I am afraid I see no other possibilities,” Ducky replies, making Tony shake his head.

 

“Look, I don’t know what to tell you, all of you, but as much as I’ve enjoyed today’s pranks, I wasn’t the guy behind them”, Tony begins to defend himself, only to get a scoff from Gibbs.  Turning his head, Tony watches as Gibbs doesn’t give any other reaction. He looks at Ducky again before turning towards his bag. “You know what to do,” he tells his team as he ducks down.  Expecting a much lighter backpack, the tug and lack of result send Tony reeling forward, stumbling not to fall right onto his face. He turns and looks at the betraying bag, ducking down to open it.

 

"Oh, very funny," he says dryly, pulling out the two dumbbells from the bag.  "See? Told y'all it wasn't me." Tony sets down the dumbbells beside his bag before he zips it shut and tosses it onto his shoulder.  "Good work today, team," he says to the now chuckling team as he walks towards the elevators, soon disappearing from sight.

 

*~*~*~*~*

 

After dinner and subsequent dishes have been taken care of, Tony slumps onto the couch, resting his head on the back of it as he feels the cushions dip beside him.  The silence is comforting and relaxing, but only lasts a few moments before Tony turns his face towards Jethro.

 

"You're good."

 

"What?" Jethro asks, looking over at him.

 

"It was all you, wasn't it?  You had help, sure, but you planned it.  All of it." Tony grins with a sparkle in his eye, as if he had just heard some very juicy gossip.

 

"I don't know what you're talking about, Tone," Jethro tells him, his hand resting just beside Tony's face with fingertips just barely brushing against his cheek.

 

"I have to ask, some of those stunts were risky, and I have no clue how you pulled them off without anyone noticing.  How?"

 

Jethro shakes his head.  "Tone, what are you going on about?" The slight, barely-there grin on his lips betrays him, however.

 

Tony laughs softly, stretching his back before sitting up, shifting his upper body towards Jethro.  “You know exactly what I’m talking about, Jeth.” He takes Jethro’s hand from the back of the couch and moves to his feet, gently guiding his husband up with him.

 

“No, I don’t.”

 

Another gentle laugh escapes Tony.  “All of today. You’re a genius. You even got Roberts.  You’ll have to make sure that our new resident geeks never find out what you can do with computers.”  He leans in and kisses Jethro’s cheek quickly before pulling back. Tony bows dramatically, grinning while he exclaims “I still have so much to learn, oh great one!”

 

The final line is what breaks Jethro, making him laugh and place his hands on Tony’s hips, pulling him close once Tony has straightened back up.  He reaches up and curls his hand around Tony’s neck, pulling him down into a kiss.
